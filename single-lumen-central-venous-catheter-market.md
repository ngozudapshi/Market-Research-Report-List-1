<p><p>The single-lumen central venous catheter market comprises various types based on the insertion site. Jugular vein catheters are inserted in the neck for quick access to the central venous system, while subclavian vein catheters are placed under the collarbone, offering stability and reduced infection risk. Femoral vein catheters are inserted in the groin, often used in emergency situations. Other types may include peripheral or specialized catheters, each designed for specific clinical needs and patient conditions, enhancing treatment options.</p></p>

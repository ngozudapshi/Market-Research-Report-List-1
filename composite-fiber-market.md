<p><p>The composite fiber market is segmented into various types based on fiber alignment and structure. Parallel composite fibers exhibit a uniform alignment, enhancing strength and durability. Cortical composite fibers mimic natural structures, offering a balance of flexibility and resilience. Island composite fibers feature a core-sheath configuration, optimizing performance for specific applications. Dispersed primary fibers combine multiple materials to achieve desired properties, allowing for versatility in use. Each type caters to different industry needs, enhancing material performance and functionality.</p></p>

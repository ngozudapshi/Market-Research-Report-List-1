<p><ul><li>Inactivated Vaccine</li><li>Live Attenuated Vaccine</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Hepatitis A vaccination market primarily consists of two types: inactivated vaccines and live attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines are made from virus particles that have been killed, offering immunity without causing disease, making them safe for all age groups. On the other hand, live attenuated vaccines use weakened forms of the virus, provoking a stronger immune response, but may not be suitable for immunocompromised individuals. Both types are essential for preventing Hepatitis A and are widely utilized in public health strategies.</p></p>

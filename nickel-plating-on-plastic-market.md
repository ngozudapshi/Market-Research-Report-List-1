<p><p>Nickel plating on plastic involves applying a layer of nickel onto plastic substrates to enhance their properties, such as conductivity, corrosion resistance, and aesthetics. There are two main market types: traditional plating, which utilizes electrochemical processes to deposit nickel, and electroless plating, where nickel is deposited without the need for electrical current, relying on chemical reactions. Both methods serve varying applications across industries, including automotive, electronics, and consumer goods, providing versatility and improved performance in engineered plastic components.</p></p>

<p><p>The livestock insurance market primarily consists of two types: Non-Commercial Mortality Insurance and Commercial Mortality Insurance. Non-Commercial Mortality Insurance covers personal livestock owned by individuals primarily for private use, protecting against unexpected deaths due to various risks. In contrast, Commercial Mortality Insurance is designed for livestock owned by businesses or farms, covering larger populations of animals and encompassing a wider range of operational risks. Both types help mitigate financial losses caused by livestock mortality while addressing different ownership contexts.</p></p>

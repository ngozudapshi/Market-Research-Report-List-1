<p><p>The silicone implant market is primarily divided into two types: silicone-filled implants and saline-filled implants. Silicone-filled implants contain a cohesive gel that mimics the natural feel of breast tissue, providing a more realistic appearance. Conversely, saline-filled implants are filled with sterile salt water; they can be adjusted for volume during surgery but may feel less natural. Both options cater to varying patient preferences and anatomical needs, influencing choices based on desired aesthetics, safety, and overall satisfaction.</p></p>
